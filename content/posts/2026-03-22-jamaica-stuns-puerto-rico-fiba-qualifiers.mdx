Jamaica's basketball journey reached a historic milestone this month, as the national team secured two back-to-back victories against perennial Caribbean powerhouses Puerto Rico in the windows of the FIBA Basketball World Cup 2027 Americas Qualifiers.

The first encounter, a 92-90 nail-biter, was decided in the final seconds of regulation. Jamaica showed immense composure under pressure, surviving a late rally by the Puerto Ricans to clinch the first win on home soil. 

## Dominating the Paint
The key to Jamaica's success in both matches was their physical dominance in the paint. The frontline, led by high-energy rebounding and rim protection, limited Puerto Rico's second-chance opportunities and controlled the pace of the game.

In the second match, Jamaica proved the first win was no fluke, delivering a 97-92 performance that showcased their growing offensive chemistry. 

## Key Takeaways
- **Resilience:** Jamaica trailed in the first half of both games but fought back with disciplined defense.
- **Bench Depth:** Important minutes from rotation players provided the scoring punch needed when the starters were in foul trouble.
- **Home Court Advantage:** The energy at the National Arena in Kingston was palpable, driving the team during critical stretches.

These wins move Jamaica up the Group B standings, putting them in a strong position as they head into the next window. While losses to Canada and a narrow 3-point defeat to the Bahamas earlier in the cycle presented challenges, this sweep of Puerto Rico signals that Jamaica is a serious contender on the regional stage.
