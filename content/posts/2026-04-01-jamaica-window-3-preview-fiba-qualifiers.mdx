After a historic sweep of Puerto Rico at home, the Jamaican national basketball team is shifting its focus to the road. The FIBA Basketball World Cup 2027 Americas Qualifiers continue this July with two massive away fixtures that will likely determine Jamaica's standing heading into the final stages of the competition.

## The Schedule
Jamaica's Window 3 schedule has been officially confirmed:

- **July 3, 2026:** Bahamas vs Jamaica (Nassau, Bahamas)
- **July 6, 2026:** Canada vs Jamaica (Toronto/Victoria, Canada)

## The Stakes
Jamaica currently sits in a competitive position in Group B, but these away games present a significant challenge. The Bahamas, who narrowly defeated Jamaica by 3 points in their previous encounter, will be playing in front of their home crowd. Jamaica's ability to contain their transition game and control the boards will be the deciding factor.

Three days later, the team travels to face Canada. As one of the top-ranked teams in the world, Canada remains the biggest hurdle in the group. Jamaica’s coaching staff will be looking to build on the lessons from their 81-97 loss in Window 1, focusing on tighter defensive rotations and consistent perimeter scoring.

## Roster Outlook
While the official roster hasn't been named, expectations are that the core group from the Puerto Rico sweep will return. There are also rumors of potential reinforcements from the professional ranks as players conclude their club seasons in Europe and North America.
